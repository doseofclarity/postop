Phases of Postoperative Recovery

🛏️ Phase I (PACU / Immediate)
• From transfer from OR until stable vital signs, awake, responsive.
• Focus: airway, breathing, circulation, pain, temperature, surgical site.
• Duration: typically 1‑4 hours.
• Aldrete score used for discharge to Phase II.
🏥 Phase II (Intermediate / Step‑down)
• Patient is awake, stable, but still requires monitoring.
• Focus: ambulation, oral intake, pain control, wound care, education.
• Discharge to home or inpatient unit.
🏡 Phase III (Extended / Home)
• Ongoing recovery after hospital discharge.
• Focus: self‑care, medication management, follow‑up appointments, activity restrictions.
• Nurse role: discharge teaching, phone follow‑up.
⚠️ Critical Phase I priorities: Maintain patent airway, prevent hypoventilation, monitor for hemorrhage, manage pain, prevent hypothermia.

Immediate Postoperative Assessment (ABCDE & Aldrete)

🌬️ Airway & Breathing
• Patency, artificial airway, breath sounds, SpO2, respiratory rate/depth.
• Signs of obstruction: stridor, gurgling, absent breath sounds.
❤️ Circulation
• Vital signs q15min, capillary refill, urine output, bleeding (drains, dressings).
• Watch for hypotension, tachycardia → hypovolemia or hemorrhage.
🧠 Disability (Neuro)
• Level of consciousness, pupil response, motor strength.
• Emergence delirium, sedation score (RASS).
🌡️ Exposure & Environment
• Temperature (prevent hypothermia), wound inspection, pain assessment (Numeric scale, FLACC).
• Nausea/vomiting, surgical drains, IV lines.
📊 Aldrete Score (discharge from PACU): Activity, Respiration, Circulation, Consciousness, O2 saturation. Score ≥9 required for transfer.

Postoperative Nursing Interventions

  • Respiratory: Incentive spirometry q1‑2h, turn/cough/deep breathing, early ambulation. Monitor for atelectasis, pneumonia.
  • Cardiovascular: Monitor I&O, daily weight, assess for DVT (SCDs, anticoagulation). Prevent hypovolemia/fluid overload.
  • GI/GU: Auscultate bowel sounds, advance diet as tolerated, monitor for nausea, urinary retention (bladder scan, catheter if needed).
  • Wound care: Assess surgical site for redness, drainage, dehiscence. Maintain aseptic technique for dressing changes.
  • Safety: Fall precautions (sedation, weakness), call light within reach, side rails up, orient patient.
Key mnemonic “P.O.S.T.” – Pain control, Oxygenation/airway, Surgical site, Thromboembolism prophylaxis.

Postoperative Complications

🚨 Early (<48 hours)
• Hemorrhage – tachycardia, hypotension, decreased Hct, bright red blood.
• Hypovolemic shock.
• Respiratory: atelectasis, aspiration, pulmonary edema.
• Urinary retention, paralytic ileus.
• Nausea/vomiting (PONV).
• Hypothermia, malignant hyperthermia.
• Emergence delirium.
⚠️ Late (>48 hours to weeks)
• Wound infection – erythema, purulent drainage, fever.
• Dehiscence / evisceration – emergency!
• DVT / PE – calf pain, swelling, sudden dyspnea.
• Pneumonia.
• Sepsis.
• Pressure injury.
• Urinary tract infection.
🆘 Evisceration emergency action: Place patient supine with knees bent, cover exposed organs with sterile saline-soaked gauze, do not attempt to reinsert, notify surgeon stat, prepare for OR.

Postoperative Pain Management

💊 Pharmacologic
• Opioids: morphine, hydromorphone, fentanyl (IV PCA).
• Non‑opioids: acetaminophen, NSAIDs (ketorolac, ibuprofen).
• Regional anesthesia: epidural, peripheral nerve blocks.
• Local anesthetics at incision site.
• Adjuvants: gabapentin, ketamine, lidocaine infusion.
🧘 Non‑Pharmacologic
• Positioning, splinting incision during cough.
• Cold therapy, relaxation, guided imagery.
• Music therapy, distraction.
• Early ambulation (reduces pain from gas/immobility).
• Acupuncture/TENS (adjunct).
📏 Pain assessment: Use numeric (0‑10) or behavioral (FLACC) scale. Reassess after intervention within 30‑60min. Goal ≤3/10.

Discharge Planning & Teaching

📋 Key discharge criteria
• Stable vital signs × 24h.
• Tolerating oral intake.
• Voiding spontaneously.
• Ambulating with minimal assistance.
• Pain controlled with oral meds.
• No active bleeding or infection.
• Family/caregiver available.
🧾 Patient teaching topics
• Incision care: keep clean & dry, signs of infection.
• Activity restrictions: no heavy lifting, driving, stairs.
• Medications: dosage, side effects, pain regimen.
• Diet: advance as tolerated, fiber to prevent constipation.
• Follow‑up appointment date.
• When to call MD: fever >38°C, increased pain, bleeding, wound separation, shortness of breath.

Flashcards & Mnemonics

Q: Aldrete score components?
Activity, Respiration, Circulation, Consciousness, O2 saturation (score 0‑2 each, total ≥9 for PACU discharge).
Q: Early signs of hemorrhage?
Tachycardia, hypotension, cool clammy skin, decreased urine output, increased drainage.
Q: Nursing action for wound evisceration?
Cover with sterile saline‑soaked gauze, place patient supine with knees bent, notify surgeon, prepare for OR.
Q: DVT prevention measures?
Early ambulation, SCDs, hydration, anticoagulants (heparin/enoxaparin), leg exercises.
Q: Most common postoperative respiratory complication?
Atelectasis (prevent with incentive spirometry, turning, coughing).
🧠 Mnemonics for recall
• “P.O.S.T.” – Pain, Oxygenation, Surgical site, Thromboembolism prevention.
• “ABCDE” – Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure.
• “DEHISCENCE” – Danger (emergency), Elevate knees, Hypovolemia prevention, Immediate notification, Sterile saline dressing, Call surgeon, Evacuate to OR.
• “R.E.L.I.E.F.” – Respiratory support, Emotional support, Latex precautions, IV fluids, Early ambulation, Fall prevention.
